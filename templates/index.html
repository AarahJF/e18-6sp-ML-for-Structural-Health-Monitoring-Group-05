The aim of this predictor is to predict the drag force coefficient for single-column-supported billboards. By analyzing the forces acting on the structure, we can provide insights into its structural safety and help optimize its design for efficiency and cost-effectiveness.

This prediction is driven by the dual goals of enhancing billboard safety through accurate drag force predictions and optimizing resource usage for cost-effective, sustainable designs.

Input Parameters

  • Clearance ratio (d/h): The clearance ratio represents the ratio of the distance between the bottom of the billboard and the ground to the height of the billboard.
  • Wind Attack Angle (in theta): The angle refers to the inclination or tilt of the billboard from the vertical position.

The drag force coefficient is a critical factor for the safety and stability of single-column billboard structures. Accurate prediction of this coefficient is vital for ensuring their durability. Engineers use this information to assess how the structure will withstand wind loads and optimize design parameters like dimensions and materials.
